Features

  • Proposal submission

    Multi-step form, PDF upload, and draft saving — resubmit as many times as needed.

  • Faculty review

    Approve, reject, or request revisions with threaded comments and instant notifications.

  • Two-stage listing

    Faculty approval auto-triggers an admin listing request. Nothing goes public without sign-off.

  • Searchable archive

    Browse and download published proposals by title, author, or year of publication.

  • Version history

    Every resubmission is saved as a snapshot tied to the feedback round that prompted it.

  • Role-based access

    Student, Faculty, and Admin — each role sees exactly what it needs, nothing more.

How it works

  1. 1

    Student submits a proposal

    Fills out the form, uploads a PDF, and submits. Drafts can be saved at any point.

  2. 2

    Faculty reviews

    A professor reads the proposal and leaves a decision — approve, reject, or request revisions with comments.

  3. 3

    Student revises and resubmits

    If revisions are requested, the student updates and resubmits. Each version is saved to history.

  4. 4

    Listing request sent automatic

    On approval, Scholarch automatically notifies the admin — no manual step needed from faculty.

  5. 5

    Admin publishes to the archive

    The admin reviews the queue and publishes or holds the proposal. Published studies are permanently accessible.
